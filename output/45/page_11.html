  10. Traveler's Review & Comparison with Wantdo Ultra Light
    Rating: 80%
    There's Ultra Light and there's ULTRA Light and when it comes to functional, packable outwear for traveling lighter is always better. How light you go depends upon the features important to you. Here, I'm comparing the Little Donkey Andy (Size Medium) to the Wantdo Women's Hooded Packable Ultra Light Weight Short Down Jacket (Size Large). The jackets (despite the different sizes) fit almost identically. Each have two zip hand warmer pockets and each have two deep internal pockets. The Little Donkey Andy has an additional zippered chest pocket.What's good about the Wantdo (which I've owned since 2018): The jacket is truly ULTRA light. It weighs a mere 10.65 oz in its stuff sack and you can tuck it into almost any kind of bag and barely know it's there. My Wantdo has gone with me to Morocco, Portugal, Africa and all over NYC, where I live. It's good for mild days, on its own, and good as a layering piece for colder days. It's great on an airplane because planes get chilly!Less good about Wantdo: Though the zippers have not failed me, I am ginger with them (especially the hand warmer pocket zips) as they are a bit dainty (keeping the jacket's weight down) - When I zip the jacket all the way up, the exposed zipper sometimes rubs against my chin. It's a minor quibble, but noticeable.What's good about the Little Donkey: The zippers are more substantial than the Wantdo. The chest pocket easily swallows my iPhone XR which means this jacket does a better job of keeping me bag free as I wanderaround town. The jacket is warm, perhaps slightly warmer than the Wantdo, though I suspect not by much. I've worn it out on a sunny day in the upper 30's/low 40's and it didn't feel much different from the Wantdo.Less good about the Little Donkey: It's heavy and bulky enough to not make the cut for my style of travel. The jacket is twice as heavy as the Wantdo at 20.75 oz and quite a bit bulkier in its stuff sack. This by no means indicates this is a heavy jacket - it is definitely not. But the weight and bulk might matter to you if you travel like me (carryon luggage only and as light as is reasonably possible).Sizing: I'm 5'3"/128 lbs/36" bust. I tried small, medium and large in the Little Donkey and stuck with medium. Small fit but would not allow layers. The large was much too big.Final Vote: No doubt, sturdier zippers, an extra pocket and poly-fill versus down make the Little Donkey Andy jacket heavier than the Wantdo. That said, I like the jacket very much. The hood fits my small head very well. The dark grey is an attractive color. I'm keeping the Little Donkey as a good replacement for some of my worn out mild-weather jackets that are much heavier and bulkier than this one. The jacket is light enough for city dwellers like me but not light enough to go on my overseas adventures. Review by City MouseCity Mouse Posted on 4/19/23
